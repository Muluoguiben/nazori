Why it works

Practice the part you actually skip

Closes the knowing-vs-saying gap

The concept is already in your head. The reps train the missing part: getting it out of your mouth in clear English, under time pressure, the way an interviewer hears it.

Feedback on what you said, not what you meant

Whisper transcribes exactly what came out and Claude grades that. If a word got mangled, you see it — because the interviewer would have heard it too.

90 seconds, not 90 minutes

One concept, one short take, then feedback. The rep is small enough that you actually do it tonight, and the next night — instead of saving prep for a weekend that never comes.

One rep keeps the streak

Finish a single explanation and your daily streak stays alive. Skips don’t count against you. No all-or-nothing routine to fall off of — just show up and say one thing out loud.

How it works

A full rep takes about two minutes

1

Tap Start session

You get one concept prompt — a term plus a question, like “closure / Explain closure in 60 seconds, with an example.” One thing to explain, no menu to navigate.

2

Talk for up to 90 seconds

Tap Start recording and a 90-second countdown begins. Explain it as if to a senior engineer. Stop when you’re done, or let it auto-stop and auto-submit at the limit. The cap is the point: it trains concision under interview pressure.

3

Read your instant feedback

Your audio goes to Whisper for a transcript, then to Claude Sonnet 4.6 for grading: four scores (Accuracy, Structure, Language /10, plus Pace in wpm against a 110–150 target), your transcript rewritten with the key fixes in bold, and exactly 3 fixes that quote your own words.

4

Go again, skip, or stop

Tap Next for a new random prompt, Skip this concept if it’s unfamiliar (it doesn’t count against your streak), or End session. Completing at least one rep keeps the daily streak going.

What's in v0

Everything you need for one focused habit

Explanation reps, one at a time

A single concept prompt shown as term + question, tagged by topic. v0 ships 20 hand-filtered Week-1 prompts across JavaScript, TypeScript, and async. No clutter, no choosing.

90-second recorder, hard cap

In-browser recording via MediaRecorder with a live countdown, auto-stop and auto-submit at the limit, plus a manual “Stop & submit” when you finish early.

Whisper transcription, no stored audio

Speech-to-text runs server-side via OpenAI Whisper. The audio blob is never persisted — only the transcript, scores, and fixes are kept.

Four-dimension AI scoring

Claude Sonnet 4.6 scores Accuracy, Structure, and Language 1–10, plus a server-computed words-per-minute Pace against a 110–150 target, returned as strict JSON.

Corrected transcript + 3 fixes

Your own words played back with the most important fixes in bold, plus exactly three actionable corrections that quote what you actually said.

Daily streak with history

One completed rep keeps the streak; skips are ignored. Rep history is saved to Neon Postgres so it follows you across phone, Mac, and Windows.

Opinionated by design

Three choices that make it different

It grades speaking, not vocabulary

Silent vocab and flashcard drilling is deliberately left to Anki. This app does one thing: explaining concepts out loud. Keeping them separate matches when and where you actually do each kind of practice.

The transcript is the ground truth

There’s no edit-the-transcript step before grading. What Whisper heard is what gets scored — because a mishear is real signal about your pronunciation, the same as it would be with a human listener.

No reference answers to recite

v0 uses freestyle AI judgment against a cached rubric, with no model answers to memorize. You practice thinking and speaking, not reciting a script.

Honest FAQ

What it is — and what it isn't yet

How many concepts can I practice right now?
20 hand-filtered Week-1 prompts covering JavaScript core, TypeScript types, async, and functions — e.g. closure, the event loop, generics, memoization. Six weeks of curriculum (~390 terms across React, backend, system design, LLMs, and agents/RAG) are drafted, but Weeks 2–6 aren’t yet wired into live prompts.
Is this a mock interview with follow-up questions?
Not yet. v0 is explanation reps only: one prompt, you explain it, you get feedback. A mock-interview mode (multi-turn, follow-up questions, an interviewer voice, end-of-session feedback) is coming next in v1 — it doesn’t exist today.
Can I fix the transcript before it gets graded?
No, and that’s on purpose. The Whisper transcript goes straight to grading. If a word is misheard, that tells you something real about your pronunciation. There’s no edit/review step and no model answer to compare against.
Is my audio stored anywhere?
No. The audio is transcribed server-side and then discarded. Only the transcript, scores, and fixes are saved to Neon Postgres.
Can I try it without API keys?
Yes. A demo mode returns a canned transcript and evaluation so the full flow runs end to end. The public link currently runs in demo mode — the sample transcript and scores there are fixed placeholders, not real grading. Real Whisper transcription and Claude grading happen only with API keys configured.
Is this a polished product I can sign up for?
No. It’s a v0, single-user tool gated by one shared-secret cookie, with no multi-user accounts. Prompts live in a JSON file in the repo, and progress-trend analytics are deferred to a later version. It’s a personal practice rig, not a SaaS product.

Audio is never stored.
